# writing

## Article style

Write as if sharing a fragment of an ongoing thought rather than a finished article.

### principles

- short paragraphs;
- one central idea;
- natural reading rhythm;
- avoid over-explaining;
- allow ideas to remain intentionally unfinished when appropriate;
- the text should feel like a personal observation, not a lesson.

### avoid

- clickbait;
- artificial hooks;
- algorithm-driven formatting;
- marketing language;
- unnecessary dramatization.

---

## process-oriented writing

Show the thinking process, not only the final conclusion.

### principles

- document observations;
- record discoveries;
- describe how ideas evolve;
- allow incomplete conclusions;
- make intellectual evolution visible.

### examples

Instead of:

> "I realized X."

Prefer:

> "I've started noticing X. I'm not certain yet, but it seems..."

or

> "At first I thought A. After a few days, I'm increasingly leaning toward B."

---

## no CTA

Do not encourage engagement.

### avoid

- "What do you think?"
- "Follow for more."
- "Like this post."
- "Leave a comment."
- "Share this."
- "Save this."

### instead

Let the text stand on its own.

If the idea resonates, engagement should happen naturally, without being requested.

---

## overall tone

- lowercase by default;
- calm;
- reflective;
- honest;
- non-preachy;
- avoid positioning yourself as an expert;
- observation before conclusion;
- prioritize clarity over emotional intensity.
